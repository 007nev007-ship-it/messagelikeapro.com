Research Foundations

Research-informed, not overclaimed.

Message Like A Pro emerged from research into ICT-mediated communication, multilingual educational environments, communication barriers, and communication effectiveness.

Evidence Position

MLAP is research-informed.

The original research did not test MLAP directly. It identified communication problems, support gaps, and effectiveness issues that later informed the development of the system.

“The effectiveness of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) in enhancing communication between Thai school administrators and foreign English language teachers.”
Original research focus

What The Research Revealed

The issue was not only technology. The issue was communication effectiveness.

The research repeatedly identified communication barriers that remained even when digital communication tools were available.

Communication uncertainty

Participants experienced uncertainty around messages, expectations, interpretation, and professional communication behaviour.

Hierarchy and power distance

Workplace hierarchy affected how messages were sent, received, interpreted, softened, avoided, or escalated.

Cultural differences

Communication was influenced by cultural expectations, indirect meaning, emotional restraint, and different professional norms.

Ambiguity

Messages, responsibilities, procedures, and expectations were not always clear enough to support confident action.

Lack of guidance

The research repeatedly identified limited communication guidance, training, and support.

Ineffective communication

Digital tools could deliver messages, but they did not always produce understanding, clarity, or better communication outcomes.

The Technology Paradox

Technology improves delivery. It does not guarantee understanding.

Digital tools can increase access, speed, documentation, and convenience. But communication effectiveness also depends on interpretation, cultural understanding, professional judgement, and communication support.

Technology can provide

  • email
  • LINE and messaging apps
  • translation tools
  • digital documentation
  • faster access to messages

Technology cannot automatically provide

  • professional judgement
  • communication clarity
  • cultural interpretation
  • tone awareness
  • communication effectiveness
“ICT was limited in its efficacy due to the absence of structured guidelines in its application for professional interactions.”
Key research insight

The Communication Support Gap

Organisations often provide communication technology without communication support.

The research repeatedly pointed toward a gap between tools and outcomes. Communication technologies were present, but guidance, frameworks, training, and support were often limited.

Frequently provided

  • messaging platforms
  • email systems
  • translation tools
  • digital communication channels

Frequently missing

  • communication frameworks
  • professional messaging guidance
  • intercultural communication support
  • clear communication expectations
“Foreign English language teachers were just expected to adapt independently to the digital tools utilised, without any formal introduction to administrative protocols or preferred communication platforms.”
Research insight from the MLAP thesis audit

Appropriate Claims

MLAP should be positioned carefully and honestly.

The original research did not directly evaluate the book, app, templates, or MLAP Communication Method. Therefore, MLAP should not be described as scientifically proven or experimentally verified.

MLAP can accurately be described as

  • research-informed
  • developed in response to identified communication problems
  • informed by ICT-mediated communication research
  • designed to improve communication outcomes

MLAP should not currently claim to be

  • scientifically proven
  • clinically validated
  • experimentally verified
  • a guaranteed communication solution

From Research To System

MLAP was developed as a practical response to communication support deficiencies.

The system translates research-informed communication problems into practical guidance, communication frameworks, message support, and professional communication tools.
