The Communication Support Gap

Technology helps people send messages. It does not automatically help them communicate effectively.

Complex Workplaces

Multilingual educational workplaces often involve different languages, expectations, professional norms, hierarchy, and cultural communication patterns.

Communication Uncertainty

Professionals may know that they need to send a message, but still feel unsure about wording, tone, timing, structure, and professional appropriacy.

Support, Not Just Technology

MLAP provides communication frameworks, practical templates, wording support, and decision guidance for real workplace situations.

Research-Informed Communication Support

The research behind MLAP repeatedly identified communication uncertainty, ambiguity, hesitation, hierarchy, unclear expectations, and the need for clearer communication support within multilingual educational workplaces.


The central finding is simple: communication technology improves message delivery, but it does not automatically improve communication effectiveness. MLAP exists inside that support gap.

The MLAP Model

MLAP operates within the communication support layer between workplace technology and communication outcomes.

1. Communication Environment

Real workplaces include role expectations, cultural differences, multilingual communication, professional pressure, and hierarchy.

2. Communication Technology

Email, messaging apps, translation tools, and workplace platforms make communication faster and easier to send.

3. Communication Support

MLAP provides wording, structure, templates, tone control, and practical decision support.

4. Communication Effectiveness

Better support can help messages become clearer, more appropriate, more professional, and easier to act on.

5. Communication Outcomes

The goal is clearer workplace communication, reduced ambiguity, better coordination, and stronger professional relationships.
