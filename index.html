The Problem

Communication problems are not always language problems.

In multilingual workplaces, communication can be affected by hierarchy, ambiguity, cultural expectations, pressure, timing, tone, and interpretation.

Unclear expectations
Hierarchy and power distance
Communication hesitation
Ambiguous messages
Tone and interpretation risk
Lack of communication guidance

Research Foundation

Built from the investigation of ICT-mediated communication.

The original research investigated the effectiveness of ICT in enhancing communication between Thai school administrators and foreign English language teachers.

The findings revealed a wider issue: technology may improve delivery, speed, and access, but it does not automatically improve understanding, interpretation, or communication effectiveness.

The MLAP Communication Method

Professional communication under pressure needs structure.

The MLAP Communication Method supports calmer, clearer, and more professionally appropriate communication in situations involving uncertainty, hierarchy, cultural difference, and communication risk.

Explore The Method

The method supports:

  • Clarifying the real situation
  • Choosing appropriate tone
  • Identifying communication risk
  • Structuring professional messages
  • Improving communication outcomes
  • Reducing avoidable misunderstandings
